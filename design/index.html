▸ 01 · WHY LOOM
"

Don't just tell the computer what to do. Explain to a person what you want the computer to do — and let the code follow the explanation.

A loom is that explanation, made real. Prose and code, woven in the order you would read them — then tangled into the source files the compiler sees.

The prose sits beside the code it describes, so the two cannot drift. The book is the program.

— after Knuth, Literate Programming, 1984
